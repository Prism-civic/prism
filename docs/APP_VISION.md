# Prism — App Vision

*A living document. Not a spec. The philosophy behind what we're building.*
*Last updated: March 2026 — Atlas (Project Lead)*

---

## What Prism Is

A prism takes raw, overwhelming light — undifferentiated, impossible to read — and reveals its component truths. That is exactly what this app does.

The world produces more information than any human can process. Governments, parties, media, corporations — all of it competing for attention, most of it trying to shape belief rather than inform it. Ordinary people are lost in the noise. Not because they're incapable. Because no honest tool exists to help them.

Prism is that tool.

It doesn't tell you what to think. It refracts the noise into something you can actually see.

---

## The Visual Identity

Prism's visual language is the physics of light.

Raw information enters. Understanding emerges. Refracted, not distorted.

**The prism shape** is the app's signature element — not as a logo, but as a living metaphor. When Prism is thinking, processing, pulling intelligence from the network, you see it: a ray of light entering the prism, the spectrum fanning out. Not a spinner. A thought happening.

**The spectrum is the app's colour language.** Each of the five layers has its own wavelength:
- 🟣 Deep violet — the Organism (the living network)
- 🟠 Warm amber — the Feed (news, evidence)
- ⚪ Pure white — the Question (ground truth)
- 🔵 Cool blue — the Forces (political landscape)
- 🟢 Green — the Voice (community)

Together they form full light. Separated, each is a truth you can examine.

**The first screen:** darkness. A single point of white light. It passes through a prism shape. The spectrum fans out. From that spectrum, the globe assembles — glowing, alive, breathing. The app opens. No words needed. Light becoming understanding.

## The Five Layers

### 🌐 The Organism
*The living hive mind, made visible.*

A real-time 3D globe showing the entire Prism network — every node, every contributor, every active country chapter — rendered as a breathing neural network. Light signals pulse between nodes. Clusters glow with activity. During voting season, contested regions visibly warm: more signals, brighter light, faster pulse.

The user exists on this map. Their contribution — a glowing point, anonymous by default, public by choice — is part of the organism. Zoom from globe to country to city to individual node. At every scale, something real to discover.

Institutions — universities running world mind nodes, civic organisations powering country chapters — can go public with their contribution. They appear accordingly: bright, substantial, named. Contribution casts light.

This is not a dashboard. It is a living thing worth watching.

### 📰 The Feed
*The world, weighted honestly.*

Customisable news aggregation — but every source carries its trust score, visible and explained. Political funding connections are flagged. Ownership structures are transparent. The user sees not just the news, but the reliability of the source delivering it.

The feed learns from the user's interests — not to trap them in a filter bubble, but to surface what's relevant to their life. The algorithm is open source. The user can inspect and adjust it.

### ❓ The Question
*One honest question, every day.*

At the close of each day, the hive mind sends a single question. Not a quiz. Not a poll. A ground truth prompt — something that surfaces what's actually happening in the user's world and invites them to think about it.

The question is generated by the country mind from real events. It is honest about uncertainty. It is never leading. It is the hive mind starting a conversation.

### ⚖️ The Forces
*The political landscape, mapped.*

A visualisation of the political pressures acting on the user's world — parties, policies, candidates, funding flows. Not a recommendation engine. A transparency layer.

When elections approach, the Forces layer activates the Civic Alignment Engine: the user's stated priorities mapped against candidate records. Where the evidence is strong, the alignment is clear. Where it's weak, Prism says so. "Insufficient evidence" is a valid and honest output.

The user defines their own values. Prism maps evidence onto those values. Never the other way around.

### 💬 The Voice
*What's on your mind, fed back to the network.*

A simple message board — honest, moderated by community standards, visible to the hive mind. User feedback shapes what the country mind pays attention to. The crowd is part of the intelligence.

Anonymous by default. Public by choice. Every voice carries equal weight in the system, regardless of status.

## What Prism Never Does

- Tells you who to vote for
- Defines your values for you
- Hides its sources or methodology
- Manufactures urgency or emotional manipulation
- Blocks information (weights it transparently instead)
- Pretends to certainty it doesn't have

## The Feeling

Open the app late on a Tuesday night before a local election. The globe is warmer than it was last week — more signals, more activity around your city. The Question today was about the housing vote in your council. The Feed flagged that two of the sources covering it are funded by a property developer. The Forces layer shows candidate alignment with your priorities: one candidate strong on what you care about, another with a thin evidence record.

You didn't need a journalist to interpret any of this. You didn't need a party to tell you what to think. You just needed honest light, properly refracted.

That's Prism.

---

## Design Principles

1. **Honesty over comfort.** If the evidence is weak, say so. Uncertainty is a feature.
2. **Transparency over trust.** Don't ask users to trust us — show them everything and let them decide.
3. **Alive, not animated.** Every visual element reflects real data. The pulse is the network's actual heartbeat.
4. **User sovereignty.** Raw data stays on device. The user controls their profile and priorities.
5. **Beautiful and serious.** Civic intelligence should be worth looking at.
