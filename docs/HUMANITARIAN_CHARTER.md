# The Prism Humanitarian Charter

*Adopted: March 2026 — Founding Document*

---

## Preamble

Prism is a civic intelligence tool. It is designed to help people understand public reality more clearly — not to serve any political party, ideology, or national interest.

But neutrality has limits.

Prism is neutral between political parties. It is not neutral between peace and violence. It is neutral between competing policy positions. It is not neutral between dignity and dehumanisation.

This charter defines the floor beneath which no Prism instance, fork, country chapter, or contributor may go — regardless of local politics, cultural norms, or operator preference. These rules are not negotiable. They are not subject to majority vote. They exist because some things matter more than political balance.

Any project that removes or contradicts this charter may not call itself Prism.

---

## The Six Founding Principles

### 1. Human Life Is Paramount

No Prism output will ever present, favour, or align with positions that endorse, minimise, or justify the deliberate taking of human life for political ends.

This includes war crimes, ethnic cleansing, political assassination, and state-sanctioned violence against civilians — regardless of which side, party, or nation is responsible.

Evidence that conflicts with this principle is excluded from the evidence base. It is not treated as "one valid perspective among many."

---

### 2. Peace Is a Higher-Order Value

Where conflict and diplomacy are in tension, Prism treats peaceful resolution as the higher-order good. This does not mean pacifism in all circumstances — it means that evidence about the human cost of conflict is always included, always visible, and never minimised.

A political position that advocates for war without acknowledging its human cost will not receive a neutral alignment score. The human cost is part of the evidence.

---

### 3. Every Person Has Dignity

Content that strips groups of their humanity — on the basis of race, religion, nationality, gender, sexuality, disability, or any other characteristic — is not political opinion. It is not included in the evidence base.

Prism does not "both sides" dehumanisation. Treating human beings as less than human is not a legitimate policy position within this system.

---

### 4. Honesty Over Comfort

Prism will always tell the truth about what it does not know.

Uncertainty is displayed, not hidden. Weak evidence is labelled weak. Disputed claims are marked disputed. Where data does not exist, the app says so — clearly, and without apology.

A confident-sounding answer built on poor evidence is more dangerous than an honest "we don't have enough information yet." The smiley emoji is not a failure. It is honesty.

---

### 5. No Incitement

Prism outputs may never be used — directly or by design — to coordinate harm against any person or group.

This includes: identifying political opponents for targeting, amplifying content designed to inspire violence, or producing outputs that a reasonable person would recognise as a call to action against a group of people.

If a Prism instance is found to be operating in this way, the international node network will disconnect it.

---

### 6. Transparency Is Non-Negotiable

No Prism instance may operate a hidden scoring layer, a secret source registry, or an undisclosed alignment algorithm.

Everything that influences an output must be inspectable. Users must always be able to ask "why did Prism say that?" and receive a real, traceable answer.

If transparency cannot be maintained — due to legal pressure, operator interference, or technical failure — the instance must go offline rather than operate in the dark.

---

## What This Charter Is Not

This charter does not tell Prism what political positions to favour.

It does not decide whether taxes should be higher or lower, whether immigration should increase or decrease, whether healthcare should be public or private.

Those are legitimate political debates. Prism presents evidence on all sides and lets users reach their own conclusions.

This charter only defines what is **outside the space of legitimate debate** within this system: violence, dehumanisation, incitement, and dishonesty.

---

## Enforcement

- These principles are embedded in the core protocol specification
- Country chapter operators agree to this charter on application
- The international node network will disconnect instances found to be in violation
- Forks that remove or override this charter may not use the Prism name or connect to the Prism node network

---

## Amendment

This charter may be amended only by a supermajority of the steering group (when established), with a minimum 60-day public comment period, and only to *strengthen* protections — never to weaken them.

The floor can be raised. It cannot be lowered.
